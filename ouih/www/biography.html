About

Born in 1996 to a mother in the banking industry and a father who was an avid photographer, I never truly became interested in 401k plans. But I did gain much influence from my father educationally and genetically. This was because on my father's side of the family, I was genetically chosen to have eyes that had extremely large orbital nerves and an extreme sensitivity to light.

It isn't life threatening by any means. It just meant that I would visit my optometrist quite often and my eyes would forever be extremely sensitive to light and color as well as I was on severe watch for glaucoma. What seemed to be a hinderence created a style I call "hyper-desaturation." A method that looks more pleasing to my eye that desaturates images to nearly a monochromatic color, but is not quite black and white. I preferred doing this, because my images were always a personal reference for if my disorder worsened into glaucoma.

I don't see my genetic disadvantage as a form of hindrance in my photographs or my photography skill, I see it as a chance to prove myself wrong and prove that we can overcome mental and physical disorders and triumph over what professionals tell us is "unavoidable."
